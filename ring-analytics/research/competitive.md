# Competitive Positioning: RingAnalytics

## Positioning Statement
RingAnalytics is the only AI call analytics platform built specifically for optometry, with eyecare-trained call scoring, marketing attribution down to the booking, and staff coaching tools — all HIPAA-compliant out of the box.

## Three Threat Levels

### Tier 1: Direct "Healthcare-First" Competitors

#### vs. Patient Prism
**Their pitch:** "AI call analytics for dental and medical. Near real-time Re-engage alerts."
**Our counter:** Patient Prism started in dental (DSOs) and expanded to general medical. Their AI is trained on dental conversations, not optometry. They don't understand eye exam scheduling cycles, optical order inquiries, contact lens follow-ups, or the specific insurance questions optometry patients ask. Their pricing is enterprise-level, designed for large dental groups. RingAnalytics is built on data from 189 optometry practices — our AI speaks your language.

#### vs. Call Box (formerly Century Interactive)
**Their pitch:** "AI + human reviewer call scoring with coaching reports."
**Our counter:** Call Box uses a hybrid model (AI + human review), which means higher costs and slower turnaround. Their reports are heavy-duty but designed for automotive and general medical — not optometry workflows. They have an aggressive sales team but no optometry-specific features. RingAnalytics is fully automated AI scoring trained on optometry call data, with instant results and lower cost.

### Tier 2: Ecosystem Competitors (Already Inside the Practice)

#### vs. Weave
**Their pitch:** "One platform for phone, texting, reviews, and reminders."
**Our counter:** Weave has basic call recording and "missed call" tracking, but their analytics are thin. They cannot score calls as Booked/Not Booked, identify new vs. returning patients, or attribute calls to specific marketing channels. A doctor might say "I already pay for Weave and it records my calls" — but recording without scoring is just a filing cabinet. RingAnalytics turns recordings into revenue intelligence.

#### vs. Solutionreach / SR Health
**Their pitch:** "Patient communication platform deeply integrated into the EHR."
**Our counter:** Solutionreach focuses on the patient journey (recalls, reviews, reminders) but has minimal call analytics. No call scoring, no marketing attribution, no transcription-based coaching. They're a communication tool, not a revenue intelligence tool.

### Tier 3: General Tech Competitors

#### vs. CallRail
**Their pitch:** "The world's most popular call tracking for SMBs."
**Our counter:** CallRail is cheap, reliable, and has solid GMB/Google Ads integration. It's what marketing agencies put on practices to prove their own ROI. But CallRail is generic — it doesn't score calls as booked/not-booked, doesn't identify new vs. returning patients, doesn't understand optometry terminology, and HIPAA compliance is an enterprise add-on (not included by default). If your marketing agency has you on CallRail, you're tracking calls. With RingAnalytics, you're tracking revenue.

#### vs. Invoca / CallTrackingMetrics (CTM)
**Their pitch:** "Enterprise Conversation Intelligence / budget call tracking."
**Our counter:** Invoca is powerful but designed for enterprise (Fortune 500) — far too complex and expensive for a 1-2 doctor optometry practice. CTM is affordable but generic with no healthcare specialization. Neither understands optometry call patterns or provides the practice-level coaching tools that RingAnalytics offers.

## Three Things Only RingAnalytics Does
1. **Optometry-Trained AI Scoring:** Trained on call data from 189 optometry practices. Understands exam types, optical orders, contact lens inquiries, and insurance-specific conversations that generic tools miss.
2. **Marketing → Booking Attribution:** Not just "which channel drove the call" but "which channel drove the BOOKING." Source + Medium + Call Score = complete marketing ROI.
3. **Exception-Based Reporting:** Flags only the calls that matter — "Not Booked" calls from "New Patients" — so owners review 3-4 lost opportunities instead of 100 calls. Five-minute weekly review that's worth thousands.
