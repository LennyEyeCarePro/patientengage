## Case Study: "The Map Pack Is the New Front Door"

### The Problem: Data Fragmentation

Most optometry practices set up their online listings once and never touch them again. Then life happens:

- The office moves. Google gets updated. Yellowpages still shows the old address.
- Hours change for summer. Facebook gets updated. MapQuest still says you close at 5.
- A new phone number replaces the old one. The website gets updated. Manta, SuperPages, and DexKnows still show the number that now rings a disconnected line.

The result is **Data Fragmentation**: one directory says "Vision Center," another says "Optometry Associates," and another has an old suite number. The practice doesn't notice because nobody checks 50+ directory websites. But Google notices.

Google uses a "Trust Score" based on NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistency across the web. When Google finds conflicting data, it loses confidence. Lower confidence means lower local ranking. Google hides the practice to protect its own reputation for accuracy.

The practice drops from #3 to #7 on the Map. At #7, they're below the fold on mobile. They're effectively invisible.

### The Impact: 10-15 Lost Exams Per Month

The local Map Pack is the primary engine for patient acquisition, capturing roughly 80% of mobile search conversions [PROVIDED]. A practice ranking #7 instead of #3 is losing at least 10 to 15 high-value eye exam bookings every month to the top three spots [PROVIDED]. That is thousands of dollars in lost chair time and optical capture every single month.

And none of it generates a notification. The practice never knows the patients they didn't get.

**Industry context:**
- 46% of all Google searches have local intent [ESTIMATE — BrightLocal/industry research, 2024]
- The Google Map Pack captures approximately 44% of clicks on local search results pages [ESTIMATE — industry research, 2024-2025]
- NAP consistency is a foundational ranking factor for local SEO, accounting for an estimated 10-15% of local ranking signals [ESTIMATE — Local SEO research]
- 81% of consumers use Google to research local businesses, including healthcare [ESTIMATE — BrightLocal, 2024]

### The Manual Alternative

Fixing Data Fragmentation manually means:
1. **Find** all the directories where the practice is listed (50+ sites, many the practice has never heard of)
2. **Claim** each listing individually (create accounts, verify ownership)
3. **Update** NAP data on each site, one at a time
4. **Discover** that some directories are actually data aggregators that feed dozens of downstream sites. Fix the aggregator, and the downstream sites eventually correct themselves. Miss the aggregator, and the wrong data keeps regenerating.
5. **Repeat** every time anything changes: new hours, new phone, new doctor, new address
6. **Remember** to do it again next time (spoiler: nobody remembers)

Estimated time: 10-20 hours for the initial cleanup, 2-5 hours for each subsequent change. At $22/hour for a staff member, that's $220-440 just for the first pass. And it doesn't stay fixed. Data drifts. Aggregators overwrite corrections. It's a maintenance job with no end.

Most practices never do this. The data just stays wrong, silently costing them visibility and patients.
