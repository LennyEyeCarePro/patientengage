# Step 6: Pre-Gen Q&A — Recalls & Reminders

## FAQ / Objection Handling

### Q1: "My EHR already sends appointment reminders. Why do I need this?"
**A:** EHR reminders typically send a single email before an appointment. PatientEngage sends up to 6 touches across SMS, email, and call — starting 7 days before and ending 2 hours before your appointment. More importantly, EHR systems don't do **recalls** — the proactive re-engagement of patients who are overdue for their annual exam. That's where the real revenue recovery happens. Our data shows 431 overdue patients rebooked in a single month across 23 practices. Your EHR can't do that.

### Q2: "Won't patients think automated texts are spam?"
**A:** Only 1% of patients opt out (Do Not Contact). Patients expect and appreciate reminders from their healthcare provider — especially via text, which is their preferred communication channel. Every message is personalized with their name, your practice name, their doctor, and their specific appointment details. Messages only arrive during business hours (you set the window). And every message includes an easy opt-out. In our data, 59% of patients confirm their appointment via automated messages — they're clearly engaged, not annoyed.

### Q3: "We don't have good email/phone data for all our patients."
**A:** You're right — our data shows about 20% of patients have incomplete contact info. But that means 80% of your patients CAN be reached automatically, right now. And the system helps you identify the gap: the "No Contact Info" alert tells you exactly which patients are missing data, so you can capture it at their next visit. Even reaching 80% of patients automatically is transforming compared to manual calling, where you might reach 30%.

### Q4: "How is this different from Solutionreach or Weave?"
**A:** Three key differences. First, PatientEngage is **built exclusively for optometry** — we have recall types like Order Pickup and Pre-Appointment Information (insurance card reminders, dilation prep) that generic healthcare platforms don't offer. Second, our multi-touch sequences go deeper — up to 6 touchpoints per recall type with intelligent channel alternation, not just 2-3 basic messages. Third, it's **integrated with your PatientEngage platform** — Scheduler, Google Reserve, Reviews — one dashboard, one login, one support team. Weave requires buying their entire phone system. Solutionreach is a separate platform with separate training.

### Q5: "What happens when a patient doesn't respond to any of the recall messages?"
**A:** The system escalates through channels over time. For Exam Recall, it starts with a gentle email at 10 months, moves to SMS at 12 months, and can add a robocall and another email at 14 months. If a patient doesn't respond to any channel after the full sequence, they're marked for staff review — but the system has already done the heavy lifting. In practice, the multi-touch approach captures the majority of reachable patients. The ones who don't respond to 4 different messages over 4 months are genuinely unreachable or have moved on.

### Q6: "How long does it take to set up?"
**A:** The recall templates are pre-built with best-practice timing and messaging for optometry. Your CSM activates them during onboarding. You can customize the timing, messaging, and channels for each touchpoint, but most practices use the defaults because they're based on what works across our network. Toggle on, and messages start flowing automatically based on your patient data. There's no complex setup, no IT involvement, and no staff training needed.

### Q7: "Do patients actually book from a text message?"
**A:** Yes. SMS has a 98% open rate (vs. 20% for email). Our messages include a direct booking link that takes the patient straight to your online scheduler. In one month, 431 patients rebooked their overdue exam and 821 rescheduled their appointment — all through automated messages. The key is meeting patients where they are: on their phone, with a one-tap booking link.

### Q8: "What about HIPAA compliance?"
**A:** All messages are HIPAA-compliant. We don't include specific health information in SMS messages. Appointment reminders contain only the minimum necessary information (date, time, practice name). Patient data is encrypted in transit and at rest. Opt-out is honored immediately. Our platform has been designed from the ground up to meet healthcare communication requirements.

### Q9: "Can I customize when messages go out?"
**A:** Completely. Each touchpoint in the sequence has three configurable settings: **timing** (how many hours/days/months before or after the trigger), **delivery days** (weekdays only, everyday, or specific days), and **delivery window** (start and end times). For example, you can set SMS to go out only on weekdays between 9AM and 5PM, while emails can go anytime. You can also turn individual touchpoints on or off within a sequence — want the 7-day email but not the 4-day text? Just toggle it.

### Q10: "What about patients who want to reschedule, not cancel?"
**A:** The system makes rescheduling easy. When patients receive a reminder, they can reply to reschedule or click the booking link to pick a new time. In one month, 821 patients rescheduled their appointments through automated messages — that's 821 appointments that would have been lost without the system. A rescheduled appointment is still a kept appointment. The system captures that intent and keeps the patient in your pipeline.

### Q11: "We're a small practice — is this only for large groups?"
**A:** The system works for any size practice. A single-location practice benefits just as much — possibly more, because you have fewer staff to dedicate to manual calling. The pre-built templates mean you get the same sophisticated multi-touch sequences that a 20-location group uses, without needing a dedicated marketing team. In our network, practices of all sizes use the same recall types.

### Q12: "What about Order Pickup notifications — how do those work?"
**A:** When a patient's glasses or contacts order arrives, the system sends an automated pickup notification via SMS and email. This is unique to PatientEngage — generic reminder tools don't handle optical orders. The Order Pickup recall type has 2 SMS + 1 Email touchpoints. For optical shops, this reduces the staff time spent calling patients about ready orders and gets patients in faster to pick up their eyewear — which also creates opportunities for add-on sales and follow-up care.
