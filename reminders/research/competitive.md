# Competitive Positioning: Recalls & Reminders

## Positioning Statement
PatientEngage Recalls & Reminders is the only patient communication system built exclusively for optometry, with eyecare-specific recall types (Order Pickup, Pre-Appointment Information), multi-touch sequences of up to 6 touchpoints, and native integration with the PatientEngage platform — at a fraction of the cost of standalone solutions.

## Head-to-Head Competitive Positioning

### vs. Solutionreach
**Their pitch:** "Complete patient communication platform for healthcare."
**Our counter:** Solutionreach is a generic healthcare platform that treats optometry the same as dentistry, dermatology, or urgent care. They don't have Order Pickup notifications, Pre-Appointment eye care prep, or recall timing calibrated to annual eye exams. Their setup takes weeks and requires dedicated onboarding staff. PatientEngage is purpose-built for optometry, with pre-configured templates that work out of the box. And it's not another platform to log into — it's integrated with your Scheduler and Google Reserve.

### vs. Weave
**Their pitch:** "One platform for phone, texting, reviews, and reminders."
**Our counter:** To use Weave's reminder features, you have to buy their entire phone system. That's a $300-600/month commitment and a phone system replacement project. PatientEngage Recalls & Reminders works independently — no phone system purchase required, no hardware to install, no phone number porting. Our multi-touch sequences go deeper (6 touches for appointment reminders vs. Weave's 2-3), and we offer recall types Weave doesn't: No Show Recovery, Order Pickup, and Pre-Appointment Information.

### vs. Demandforce
**Their pitch:** "Patient communication and marketing automation."
**Our counter:** Demandforce was built around email marketing in an era before patients lived on their phones. Their SMS capabilities were bolted on later. PatientEngage is SMS-first — because 98% of texts are opened vs. 20% of emails. Our multi-touch sequences alternate channels intelligently, starting with email for detail and escalating to SMS for action. Demandforce is also owned by Internet Brands (WebMD's parent company), raising questions about how patient data is used.

### vs. EHR Built-in Reminders
**Their pitch:** "It's already included with your practice management system."
**Our counter:** EHR reminders send a single email before an appointment. That's it. No SMS. No recall sequences for overdue patients. No no-show follow-up. No order pickup. No multi-touch cadences. Our data shows 59% of calls go to voicemail — if your EHR is phone-based, you're missing most patients. PatientEngage sends up to 6 touches across 3 channels, covering the entire patient lifecycle from recall to confirmation to no-show recovery. Your EHR does appointment reminders. We do patient retention.

## Three Things Only PatientEngage Does
1. **Order Pickup Notifications:** When a patient's glasses or contacts arrive, automated SMS + email tells them to come pick them up. No other reminder platform handles optical orders.
2. **Pre-Appointment Information:** Insurance card reminders, dilation expectations, what-to-bring checklists — sent automatically before eye exams. Generic platforms don't know your pre-visit workflow.
3. **Unified Platform Integration:** Recalls & Reminders works natively with PatientEngage Scheduler (bookings flow directly), Google Reserve (patients who book online get the same reminder sequences), and Reviews (post-visit review requests). One platform, not a stack of disconnected tools.

## Price Advantage
PatientEngage Recalls & Reminders is part of the PatientEngage platform subscription. Competitors charge $200-600/month as standalone products. For a practice already on PatientEngage, recalls and reminders are included — not an add-on with a separate bill.
