Common questions

Things practice owners ask about Recalls and Reminders.

Every question below is one we hear regularly. Here is the honest answer to each one.

"We already send recall postcards. Why would I need this?" ↓
Postcards have a response rate of about 1-3%. A personalized text message with a direct booking link gets opened within minutes and makes it possible to book an appointment with one tap. PatientEngage does not replace postcards if you want to keep sending them. It adds a high-conversion digital channel that works faster, costs less, and tracks every response automatically.
"I do not want to annoy patients with too many messages." ↓
The system sends a carefully timed sequence, not a blast. Each patient receives one text and one email, spaced appropriately. If a patient books or replies, they are removed from the sequence automatically. The tone is warm, personal, and professional. Patients who are actually overdue for an eye exam appreciate being reminded. The ones who book within hours of receiving the text are proof of that.
"My front desk already calls overdue patients when they have time." ↓
That is the problem. "When they have time" means it happens inconsistently, gets deprioritized when the day is busy, and covers a fraction of overdue patients. PatientEngage contacts every overdue patient, every night, automatically. The front desk does not need to find time because the system never stops running. Their time is better spent on the patients already in the office.
"How does it know which patients are overdue?" ↓
PatientEngage connects directly to your EHR and reads appointment history, last visit dates, and recall schedules nightly. It uses the same data your staff would use if they were pulling a recall list manually. The difference is that the system does it every night without anyone asking, and it never misses a patient or falls behind.
"What if a patient has already rebooked somewhere else?" ↓
If a patient has a future appointment in your EHR, they are automatically excluded from the recall list. The system only contacts patients with no upcoming appointment who are past their due date. If a patient replies and says they have moved or found another doctor, the system logs their response and removes them. No one gets a reminder they should not.
