## Q7: Top Objections (from Lenny : critical insights)

### Objection 1: Loss of Customer Data and Relationship
- **Customer Ownership fear:** When bookings are made through Google, the customer is treated as a "Google Customer" rather than a direct customer of the business
- **Inhibits loyalty and marketing:** Reduces ability to build loyalty, email marketing efforts, or manage the customer relationship directly
- **Reduced Direct Web Traffic:** By facilitating booking directly on search results, users have no reason to visit the practice website, reducing opportunities for upselling or promoting other services
- **PatientEngage response:** The Book online button can link to ANY page, including the practice's own website! Unlike Zocdoc, the practice owns the patient relationship. When using PE Scheduler, patient data goes directly to the practice. When using 3rd party link, traffic goes to the practice's own page.

### Objection 2: "Hijacking" and Unauthorized Activation
- **Unauthorized Third-Party Access:** Eye care facilities have reported that systems (such as Zocdoc) have activated the service without proper consent from the venue
- **PatientEngage response:** Google Reserve in PatientEngage is a TOGGLE that the practice controls. Nothing happens without the practice explicitly turning it on. The practice chooses where the button links. No third party can activate it on their behalf.

### Objection 3: Fear of Higher No-Show Rates
- **Perception:** Scheduling through a quick button on Google might lead to more impulsive, less committed appointments
- **Higher no-shows:** Compared to patients who fill out a detailed appointment request form on the official practice website
- **PatientEngage response:** Google Reserve links to the SAME booking flow (PE Scheduler or practice page). The patient still fills out the same information. The only difference is they got there in one click instead of three. Intent from a Google search is HIGH, not impulsive.

## Q8: Internal Data on Click-to-Appointment Conversion
**Answer:** No, clicks only. PatientEngage tracks clicks on the Book online button, but not downstream conversions from those clicks to actual booked appointments. When using 3rd party booking links, tracking ends at the click.
