# Step 5 : Competitive Research: Google Reserve for Optometry

## Market Context
Reserve with Google allows patients to schedule eye care and health appointments directly through Google Search and Maps without leaving the page. This feature works through integrations with major healthcare scheduling platforms.

## Tier 1: Direct Competitors (Optometry/Eye Care Focused)

### 1. EyeCarePro
**Category:** Certified Google partner for optometry
**What they do:** Handles the technical setup for optometry practices to show "Book Now" buttons on their Google Business Profiles. Specifically focused on vision care.
**Strengths:**
- Certified Google partner designation (official)
- Purpose-built for optometry practices
- Handles full technical setup for practices
- Established presence in eye care vertical
**Where they fall short:**
- Focused primarily on the Google integration, not a full patient engagement platform
- Does not offer AI Voice Agent, campaign management, recalls/reminders, or real-time scheduler
- Technical setup approach vs. PatientEngage's simple toggle
- No network-level reporting (PatientEngage tracks 227,000+ clicks across 1,200+ locations)
**Key differentiator:** Google-certified optometry specialist

### 2. Practice by Numbers
**Category:** Booking platform for vision and dental centers
**What they do:** Provides a "Book Online" button specifically for vision and dental centers that syncs directly with their practice management systems.
**Strengths:**
- Direct PMS sync (real-time availability)
- Dual-vertical: vision + dental
- Focus on data and analytics for practices
**Where they fall short:**
- Narrower platform (booking-focused, not full patient engagement)
- No AI voice agent or campaign management
- Less flexibility in booking link destination
- Smaller network footprint
**Key differentiator:** Direct PMS sync for real-time availability

### 3. Reservio
**Category:** Vision center appointment software
**What they do:** Used by opticians to increase visibility on Google and allow patient self-booking.
**Strengths:**
- Self-booking focus reduces admin burden
- Google visibility tools
- Optician-specific workflows
**Where they fall short:**
- Smaller platform, less established
- Limited to booking functionality
- No broader patient engagement suite
- No network-wide data or reporting
**Key differentiator:** Optician-focused self-booking

## Tier 2: General Healthcare Platforms with Google Reserve

### 4. Zocdoc
**Category:** Major health scheduling marketplace
**What they do:** Integrates with Google to provide booking for thousands of doctors and specialists. Patient-facing marketplace.
**Strengths:**
- Massive patient-facing marketplace
- Google integration at scale
- Brand recognition with patients
- Multi-specialty including optometry
**Where they fall short:**
- Marketplace model: patients belong to Zocdoc, not the practice
- Practice pays per booking (lead-gen model vs. SaaS)
- No practice-side tools (no scheduler, no AI voice, no campaigns)
- Cannot link to practice's own booking page (locks into Zocdoc flow)
- Practice loses direct patient relationship
**Key differentiator:** Patient-facing marketplace with massive discovery

### 5. NexHealth
**Category:** Patient experience platform for medical/dental
**What they do:** Reserve with Google integration as part of broader patient experience platform. Simple toggle in settings.
**Pricing:** Starting at $350+/month
**Strengths:**
- Native Reserve with Google integration
- Comprehensive platform (scheduling, forms, communications, payments)
- 100+ EHR/PM integrations
- Flexible month-to-month billing
**Where they fall short:**
- Higher pricing ($350+/month)
- Primarily dental/medical, not optometry-focused
- Integration routes to NexHealth booking only (less flexible than PE's any-URL option)
- No AI Voice Agent
- No network-wide GBP insights reporting
**Key differentiator:** All-in-one patient experience with built-in Google Reserve

## Tier 3: Adjacent Platforms

### 6. Jane App
**Category:** Clinical management platform
**What they do:** Used by eye care clinics to pass treatment lists and real-time availability to Google.
**Strengths:** Clinical depth, real-time availability sync
**Where they fall short:** Clinic management tool, not patient engagement platform. No marketing, campaigns, or AI capabilities.

### 7. Weave
**Category:** Dental/optometry practice management
**Pricing:** $249-$349/month
**What they do:** Online scheduling link to Google Business Profile via TrueLark integration.
**Strengths:** Strong optometry presence, affordable pricing, comprehensive communication tools
**Where they fall short:** Integration via TrueLark (not direct), no AI voice agent, less flexible URL routing

### 8. CVS MinuteClinic / HealthEngine / Medical Departures
**Category:** Large healthcare networks/marketplaces
**Relevance:** These are primary Google healthcare partners but not competitors to PatientEngage. They serve different markets (retail clinics, Australian market, medical tourism).

## PatientEngage's Core Differentiators for Google Reserve

1. **Flexibility:** Can link the "Book online" button to PE Scheduler, practice website, 3rd party scheduler, or ANY URL the practice wants. No other competitor offers this level of flexibility.

2. **Part of a complete platform:** Google Reserve is one feature within a comprehensive suite (Scheduler, AI Voice Agent, Campaigns, Reviews, Recalls, Social Posts, Directory Listings, Landing Pages, Forms). Competitors are either booking-only or require multiple vendors.

3. **Simple toggle activation:** One click to turn on, live within 24 hours. No technical setup required (unlike EyeCarePro's "handled technical setup" approach).

4. **Network-wide data:** 227,000+ Google Reserve clicks tracked across 1,200+ locations in a single month. No competitor publishes data at this scale for optometry.

5. **AI Voice Agent:** The only platform in this space with an AI phone agent, catching patients who call instead of clicking "Book online."

6. **Practice ownership:** Unlike Zocdoc's marketplace model, the practice owns the patient relationship. The booking happens on the practice's terms.

## Honest Assessment
- **EyeCarePro** is the most direct threat in optometry: they have Google certification and marketing expertise in eye care
- **Zocdoc** has the strongest patient-facing brand but their model conflicts with practice interests
- **NexHealth** has the closest feature set but lacks optometry focus and AI voice
- **Practice by Numbers** has real-time PMS sync which is compelling for practices wanting live availability
- **PatientEngage wins** on flexibility (any URL), platform breadth (AI voice, campaigns, reviews, recalls), and network scale (1,200+ locations with Google Reserve)
