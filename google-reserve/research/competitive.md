# Competitive Analysis: Google Reserve

## Core Differentiator
PatientEngage is the only platform that combines Google Reserve integration with complete flexibility on booking destination (any URL), an AI Voice Agent for phone backup, and a full patient engagement suite, all under one roof. Competitors either lock you into their booking system, treat your patients as their customers, or offer Google Reserve as a standalone feature without the broader platform.

## Competitor Breakdown

### EyeCarePro
**What they offer:** Technical setup services for optometry practices to show "Book Now" buttons on Google Business Profiles. Positioned as a Google partner for eye care.
**Strengths:** Deep optometry focus, established brand in eye care marketing, handles the technical setup for practices
**Weaknesses:** Service-based approach (they set it up for you) vs. self-service toggle, not a full patient engagement platform, no AI Voice Agent, no real-time scheduler, no built-in click tracking at network scale
**Honest take:** EyeCarePro knows optometry marketing well and has credibility in the vertical. But their Google Reserve offering is a service, not a product feature. PatientEngage makes it a one-click toggle within a broader platform.

### Zocdoc
**What they offer:** Patient marketplace that integrates with Google to show "Book" buttons. Patients book through Zocdoc's system.
**Strengths:** Massive patient-facing brand, high visibility, large provider network
**Weaknesses:** Marketplace model means the patient is Zocdoc's customer, not yours. Practices pay per booking. Practices have reported Zocdoc activating Google Reserve on listings without explicit consent. Reduces direct website traffic. Practice loses control of the relationship.
**Honest take:** Zocdoc is the biggest name in healthcare booking, but their model conflicts with practice interests. The unauthorized activation reports are a real trust issue. PatientEngage puts the practice in control.

### NexHealth
**What they offer:** Patient experience platform with built-in Reserve with Google toggle. Integration routes patients to NexHealth's online booking page.
**Pricing:** Starting at $350+/month
**Strengths:** Comprehensive platform (forms, payments, communications), 100+ EHR integrations, flexible month-to-month billing
**Weaknesses:** Higher pricing, primarily dental/medical (not optometry-focused), books only to NexHealth's system (not flexible URL), no AI Voice Agent, no network-wide GBP insights reporting
**Honest take:** NexHealth is the closest all-in-one competitor. Their Google Reserve is solid but limited to their own booking system. PatientEngage wins on flexibility (any URL), AI Voice Agent, and optometry focus.

### Practice by Numbers
**What they offer:** "Book Online" button for vision and dental centers with direct PMS sync, showing real-time availability on Google.
**Strengths:** Real-time availability sync is compelling, direct PMS integration
**Weaknesses:** Narrower platform (booking-focused), no AI voice capabilities, limited marketing/engagement features
**Honest take:** Their real-time PMS sync is a genuine strength. For practices that want live availability shown on Google, this is appealing. PatientEngage offers broader value across the full patient journey.

### Jane App
**What they offer:** Clinical management platform used by eye care clinics to pass availability to Google.
**Strengths:** Strong clinical workflow tools, real-time availability
**Weaknesses:** Clinic management tool, not a patient engagement platform. No marketing, campaigns, reviews, or AI capabilities.
**Honest take:** Jane App serves a different need. It's a practice management system that happens to connect to Google, not a marketing and engagement platform.
