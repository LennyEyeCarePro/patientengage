# Screenshot Brief — One-Click Campaigns

## Shot 1: Campaign Library (Selection Screen)
**Screen:** Campaigns page → Campaign selection library showing themed campaign tiles
**Setup:** Navigate to the campaign setup flow. Show the library view with multiple campaign themes visible (Allergies, Dry Eye, Myopia Management, etc.)
**What to highlight:** The variety of optometry-specific themes and the simplicity of the selection interface. Each tile should show the campaign name and a preview thumbnail.
**Why it matters:** This is the "one click" starting point. The viewer needs to see that creating a multi-channel campaign begins with a simple choice — not a blank canvas, not a design tool. Marketing claim: "Pick a topic. That's the hardest part."
**Data requirements:** No patient data. Practice-agnostic view.

## Shot 2: Campaign Preview — Email
**Screen:** Campaign setup flow → Preview carousel → Email piece
**Setup:** Select any campaign and advance to the preview. Show the email piece with practice branding visible (practice name, logo, booking link in the email body).
**What to highlight:** Professional design quality. Practice name and booking link auto-populated. This is a finished, branded email — not a Canva template. The visual theme should be clearly recognizable so viewers can see the same imagery carry across other pieces.
**Why it matters:** Sarah (Persona 2) needs to see output she'd be proud to send. The email is the first touchpoint that pulls patients into the ecosystem. Marketing claim: "Agency-quality email — generated, not designed."
**Data requirements:** Use a real practice (NOT SeaShore #1718). Must show practice branding merge fields populated.

## Shot 3: Campaign Preview — Landing Page
**Screen:** Campaign setup flow → Preview carousel → Landing page piece
**Setup:** Same carousel, navigate to the landing page preview. Show the full page with campaign theme imagery, practice name, CTA button, and appointment/booking action.
**What to highlight:** This is the conversion engine. The landing page keeps the visitor inside the practice's ecosystem — not bouncing to a generic website. Same imagery as the email. Clear appointment CTA. Practice contact info visible.
**Why it matters:** The landing page is what separates this from "just email marketing." It's the ecosystem closer. Coastal Eye Associates saw 10% of recipients visit the landing page. Marketing claim: "Patients don't just read the email — they land on a page built to book them."
**Data requirements:** Use a real practice. Must show booking/contact CTA. Same visual theme as Shot 2.

## Shot 4: Campaign Preview — Social Post (Facebook or GMB)
**Screen:** Campaign setup flow → Preview carousel → Facebook or GMB post
**Setup:** Navigate to a Facebook post or GMB post in the carousel. Show the post with campaign imagery and practice branding.
**What to highlight:** Same imagery and messaging as the email and landing page. The viewer should instantly recognize the visual consistency — this is the same campaign showing up on social. Practice name visible.
**Why it matters:** Proves the ecosystem story. The patient sees the email at breakfast, the Facebook post at lunch, the Google post when searching. Same message, same look. Marketing claim: "Your patients see you everywhere — because the campaign shows up everywhere."
**Data requirements:** Use a real practice. Must show visual consistency with Shots 2 and 3.

## Shot 5: Campaign Preview — Poster
**Screen:** Campaign setup flow → Preview carousel → Poster piece
**Setup:** Show the print-ready poster with campaign theme and practice branding.
**What to highlight:** Bridges digital and physical. Same visual identity as the email, landing page, and social posts — but printed for the waiting room, the optical, or the front window. The ecosystem extends into the office itself.
**Why it matters:** This is the moment the viewer realizes One-Click Campaigns isn't just "email marketing" or "social media" — it's a complete presence. Marketing claim: "Digital and in-office. Same campaign. Same message."
**Data requirements:** Use a real practice. Same visual theme as Shots 2-4.

## Shot 6: Campaign Results Dashboard
**Screen:** Campaigns → Results section for a completed campaign
**Setup:** Navigate to a completed campaign's results view. Show the metrics: Emails Sent, Unique Opens, Page Views, EHR Email Appointments. Use the case study practice or a practice with strong numbers.
**What to highlight:** Complete funnel visibility — sends → opens → landing page views → appointments. This is the proof that the ecosystem converts. The appointment number should be prominent.
**Why it matters:** The Growth Chaser (Persona 3) won't believe in marketing until they see appointment numbers. Dr. Someday (Persona 1) needs ROI to justify the investment. Marketing claim: "Every campaign shows you exactly how many appointments it drove."
**Data requirements:** MUST use a real practice with real performance data (Step 3 case study preferred). NO SeaShore data.

## Shot 7: Network-Wide Admin Report
**Screen:** Admin → Campaign Reports → Network view showing all practices
**Setup:** Show the admin report with the full practice list, sortable by emails sent, open rates, and appointments. Filter to show active practices.
**What to highlight:** The scale — 647 practices, millions of emails, thousands of appointments. This is social proof at the network level.
**Why it matters:** Dr. Someday needs to know he's not the first one trying this. Marketing claim: "647 practices are already keeping their patients close."
**Data requirements:** Network-level aggregate view. No individual practice data that could identify specific practices. NO SeaShore.

## Shot 8: The Ecosystem View (Composite/Hero Shot)
**Screen:** Composite image or side-by-side showing multiple campaign pieces together — email, landing page, Facebook post, poster — all with the same visual theme and practice branding.
**Setup:** This may be assembled as a composite from individual screenshots (Shots 2-5) or designed as a hero image. The goal is a single visual that shows "one campaign, everywhere."
**What to highlight:** Visual consistency across all channels. Same imagery, same messaging, same branding — email, landing page, social, print. This is the hero image for the marketing page.
**Why it matters:** This single shot tells the entire value proposition: your patients are surrounded by your brand between visits. Marketing claim: "Same message. Every channel. One click."
**Data requirements:** Use a real practice. Same campaign across all pieces. NO SeaShore.
