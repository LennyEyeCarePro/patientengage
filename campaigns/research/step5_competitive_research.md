## Competitive Landscape for Campaigns-To-Go

The Campaigns-To-Go feature sits at a unique intersection: it is **automated marketing campaign execution** (not manual agency work), **multi-channel** (not just email or just social), **EHR-integrated** (not generic), and **optometry-specific** (not one-size-fits-all). No single competitor occupies this exact position.

The competitive landscape breaks into four tiers, each with a different "malignancy" relative to Campaigns-To-Go.

---

## Tier 1: Specialized Eye Care Competitors

These are the most direct rivals. They "know" optometry — but their campaign capabilities are fundamentally different from Campaigns-To-Go.

### Marketing4ECPs
- **Has campaigns?** Yes — but as a **manual agency service**, not automated software.
- **Approach:** High-touch, high-design agency model. They create campaign creative, manage social media, and send email marketing on behalf of practices. Human designers and strategists produce each piece.
- **Key marketing message:** Premium agency feel; "we know eye care marketing."
- **Cost:** Agency pricing — typically $1,000–$3,000+/mo for bundled services (website + social + email). [ESTIMATE based on agency market positioning]
- **What they do better:** Custom creative quality. A dedicated human designer can produce bespoke campaign assets tailored to a specific practice's brand voice.
- **What they do worse:** Scale and automation. Every campaign requires human labor. A practice can't "schedule a campaign" at 2 AM on a Sunday. There's no EHR integration for patient targeting. The practice is dependent on the agency's bandwidth.
- **Use case difference:** Marketing4ECPs is hiring a marketing team. Campaigns-To-Go is having a marketing team built into your software.

### Roya (Canvas CMS/CRM)
- **Has campaigns?** Partial — through their "Canvas" CMS/CRM hybrid.
- **Approach:** Design-first platform. Sleek mockups and modern website templates with CRM capabilities bolted on. Campaign features are secondary to the website/CRM core.
- **Key marketing message:** Modern design + CRM integration.
- **Cost:** Platform subscription — likely $300–$800/mo for full suite. [ESTIMATE]
- **What they do better:** Modern UI/UX for the practice's website. Their CMS is polished.
- **What they do worse:** "Design-first, not conversion-first." A beautiful site that doesn't generate 7 campaign pieces automatically and doesn't talk to the EHR for targeted patient outreach is a digital trophy. No evidence of automated multi-channel campaign generation.
- **Use case difference:** Roya sells a website platform with CRM. Campaigns-To-Go is a marketing execution engine that happens to live inside a patient engagement platform.

### Glacial Multimedia
- **Has campaigns?** Partial — primarily in Ophthalmology/Refractive (LASIK, Cataract).
- **Approach:** Digital marketing agency focused on surgical eye care. Website design, SEO, PPC. Campaign work is agency-managed.
- **What they do worse for optometry:** Their strength is refractive surgery marketing. Optometry campaigns (Dry Eye, Myopia Management, Allergies) are not their core competency.
- **Use case difference:** Adjacent market. They compete more with surgical centers than independent optometry practices.

### Doctor Multimedia
- **Has campaigns?** Yes — but high-volume, low-quality.
- **Approach:** "Churn and burn" model. Cookie-cutter strategies applied across many practices.
- **The malignancy:** Practices who've been with them are often "Battle Scarred" — skeptical of marketing platforms. Low-quality execution means their campaigns lack the professional design quality observed in Campaigns-To-Go (Step 4).
- **What they do worse:** Everything about quality. The Campaigns-To-Go creative (observed in Step 4) is noticeably professional — not "small business clip art." Doctor Multimedia's output often IS the clip art.

## Tier 2: Legacy & Integration Rivals (Patient Communication Systems)

These companies provide or integrate with the EHR and try to own the patient communication journey. They are the closest functional competitors — but they compete on **recalls and reminders**, not **marketing campaigns**.

### Solutionreach
- **Has campaigns?** Partial. They have email communication capabilities focused on recalls, reminders, and patient newsletters.
- **Approach:** Patient Relationship Management (PRM). Recalls, reminders, reputation. 20+ years in the market. They focus on retaining existing patients through automated communication workflows.
- **Key marketing message:** "Patient retention and relationship management."
- **Cost:** $300–$500+/mo depending on practice size and modules. [ESTIMATE]
- **What they do better:** Recall and reminder automation. They've been doing "remind patients to come back" for two decades.
- **What they do worse:** Marketing campaigns. Their email is transactional (appointment reminders, recall notices), not promotional (seasonal campaigns, awareness months, service promotions). No multi-channel campaign generation. No automated social media posting. No campaign landing pages. No campaign posters.
- **Use case difference:** Solutionreach says "your patient is overdue, remind them." Campaigns-To-Go says "here's a compelling reason to come in this month, delivered across 5 channels simultaneously."
- **The malignancy:** They do the "plumbing" (recalls, reminders) well, but they are terrible at acquisition-focused marketing campaigns. They are transactional, not promotional.

### Weave
- **Has campaigns?** Yes — basic email marketing.
- **Approach:** All-in-one front office platform. Phone/VOIP system first, then layers on patient communication: two-way texting, email marketing, online scheduling, reviews. Email marketing is a feature within the platform, not the platform's core value.
- **Key marketing message:** "Email Marketing to boost your bottom line." Claims email generates "$38 in revenue for every dollar spent."
- **Cost:** Starting at $249/mo (Pro plan). Email marketing included with all plans.
- **What they do better:** Phone integration. Weave's VOIP + caller ID + patient context during calls is genuinely useful for front desk staff. Two-way texting is strong.
- **What they do worse:** Campaign automation. Weave requires **manual campaign creation** (though AI-assisted). No multi-channel campaign generation — email, social, and other channels are standalone tools managed separately. No automated Facebook/Google/Instagram posting tied to a campaign theme. No campaign landing pages. No campaign posters. No auto-scheduling of 14 pieces across a month from one action.
- **Use case difference:** Weave is a phone system that added email marketing. Campaigns-To-Go is a marketing campaign engine that generates 7 pieces across 5 channels automatically.

### Promptly
- **Has campaigns?** Similar to Solutionreach — focused on patient communication, not marketing campaigns.
- **The malignancy:** Same as the tier: good at plumbing (communication), not at marketing engine work.

## Tier 3: Healthcare/Medical Generalists

These companies sell to dentists, chiropractors, and every type of medical practice. They treat an optometrist like a plumber.

### Tebra (formerly PatientPop + Kareo)
- **Has campaigns?** Yes — through PatientPop's marketing platform.
- **Approach:** Website + SEO + reputation management + patient communication. High-tech, high-price, but generic medical marketing.
- **Key marketing message:** "Complete practice growth platform."
- **Cost:** $500–$1,000+/mo for the full platform. [ESTIMATE]
- **What they do better:** Scale and brand recognition. Large engineering team, polished platform.
- **What they do worse:** Optometry specificity. They don't understand the difference between a Scleral lens patient and a root canal patient. Their campaign content is generic medical content. No optometry-specific campaign themes (Dry Eye Awareness, Myopia Management, Eye Allergy Season). No EHR-integrated patient filtering for optometry-specific segments.
- **Use case difference:** Tebra sells medical practice marketing. Campaigns-To-Go delivers optometry-specific campaign themes pre-built, branded per practice, and distributed across 5 channels automatically.

### iMatrix
- **Has campaigns?** Basic — websites + some email marketing for healthcare.
- **Approach:** Budget corporate option. High-volume sales, low-touch service.
- **The malignancy:** Practices feel like a number. "Specialty" marketing (e.g., campaigns themed around specific eye conditions) is virtually non-existent.
- **What they do worse:** Campaign specificity and quality. No optometry-themed campaign library. No multi-channel automation.

## Tier 4: Generalist "Small Biz" Players

These platforms sell to everyone from florists to optometrists.

### Thryv
- **Has campaigns?** Basic email/SMS marketing for small businesses.
- **Approach:** Business management software (invoicing, scheduling, CRM) with marketing bolted on.
- **What they do worse:** Total lack of industry expertise. No optometry-specific campaigns. No healthcare compliance awareness. No EHR integration.
- **Use case difference:** Thryv is Quickbooks that sends emails. Not a marketing campaign engine.

### Marketing 360
- **Has campaigns?** Yes — through a managed marketing platform.
- **Approach:** One-size-fits-all agency platform with dedicated "marketing executives."
- **What they do worse:** No idea what a "Trunk Show" is or why "Myopia Management" matters. Campaigns would be generic small business promotions, not clinically-informed eye care campaigns.

## The True Differentiator

### What no competitor does:
**Auto-generate 7 professionally designed, practice-branded marketing pieces across 5 channels from a single click, using optometry-specific campaign themes, targeted to the practice's actual patient list via EHR integration.**

Breaking that down:
1. **7 pieces from 1 click** — No competitor generates email + landing page + Facebook posts (×4) + Google posts (×4) + Instagram post + poster + Facebook cover automatically. Agencies do it manually. Communication platforms don't do it at all.
2. **Practice-branded automatically** — Each piece pulls the practice's logo, phone, booking link, review count, and live hours. No template customization required.
3. **Optometry-specific themes** — Campaign topics are clinically relevant (Allergies, Dry Eye, Myopia Management, Diabetes, New Year Benefits). Generalists can't produce this. Communication platforms don't try.
4. **EHR-integrated targeting** — Campaigns can be filtered by patient attributes (age, last appointment, ZIP, insurance). No agency or generalist platform has this.
5. **Multi-channel auto-scheduling** — 14 individual pieces scheduled across a month (weekly social posts, email blast, cover photo swap + reset) from one action. No competitor automates this coordination.

### Differentiator Statement:
> "Competitors either design your marketing manually (agencies) or remind your patients automatically (communication platforms). PatientEngage does both — it designs AND delivers a complete multi-channel marketing campaign every month, automatically, using your actual patient list."

### Alternative framing:
> "Marketing agencies charge $1,000+/month and still need you to approve every post. Communication platforms send reminders but can't run a marketing campaign. Campaigns-To-Go gives you agency-quality campaigns that run themselves."

## Key Insight for Marketing Page

The competitive angle is NOT "we have campaigns and they don't." Many competitors have some form of campaign or marketing capability. The angle is:

**"Nobody else gives you a complete, multi-channel marketing campaign — designed, branded, scheduled, and posted — from a single action."**

The closest competitors to this claim are:
- **Agencies** (Marketing4ECPs, Roya) — they produce multi-channel campaigns, but manually, expensively, and slowly.
- **Weave** — they have email marketing, but it's one channel, manual creation, and not optometry-themed.

The farthest competitors:
- **Solutionreach** — they don't even try to do marketing campaigns. They're recalls and reminders.
- **Thryv / Marketing 360** — they have no optometry knowledge whatsoever.
