## Part A — Content Critique

### Pagebrief
**Strengths:**
- "Fire Your Marketing Agency" — creates real urgency and directly names the enemy (the agency). Strong emotional opener for Dr. Someday.
- "One Click. Seven Pieces. Five Channels. Zero Hassle." — concrete and specific. Not generic SaaS.
- Meta description at 141 chars — under 155 limit. ✅
- SEO title at 53 chars — under 60 limit. ✅

**Weaknesses found:**
1. Third headline ("Your Patients Are Already In Your Database") is weaker — it describes a situation rather than creating urgency. Could be stronger.
2. CTA options 2 and 3 are similar in tone. Option 3 ("Get Your First Campaign Running This Week") is the most actionable — should be first.

**Changes made:** None yet — presenting for review.

### Overview
**Strengths:**
- "What It Does" is clear enough that a non-technical reader could explain it.
- "Key Workflow" is 4 concrete steps, not abstract.
- "The Problem It Solves" uses persona language directly (Facebook page since 2023, job of three people, agency costs).

**Weaknesses found:**
1. "The Problem It Solves" paragraph is long (4 sentences). Could be tighter.
2. Missing: no mention of the campaign library's optometry-specific themes in "What It Does" — this is a differentiator that should appear earlier.

### Benefits
**Strengths:**
- Each benefit is tied to a specific persona (agency replacement → Dr. Someday, hours back → Sarah, specialty pipeline → Growth Chaser).
- Stats are sourced and tagged.
- Testimonial reads like a real person — not corporate.

**Weaknesses found:**
1. Benefit #5 ("Consistency that compounds") is the weakest — it's more abstract than the others. The stat is strong (647 practices, 54 appts) but the framing ("builds a habit in their patient base") is vague.
2. The testimonial is labeled "Composite — anonymized" — good for internal tracking, but this label would need to be removed before publishing. The copy itself is strong.
3. "Social Proof" section lists 5 stats — could be overwhelming. Consider leading with 2-3 most compelling.

### Competitive
**Strengths:**
- Honest about where competitors win (agencies on bespoke quality, Weave on phone integration, Solutionreach on recall depth). This is analytically honest, not self-promotional.
- Clear structure: each competitor type gets a "their pitch / the reality / our advantage" framework.
- Positioning matrix is clean and scannable.

**Weaknesses found:**
1. The "Honest Assessment" section at the bottom is good for internal credibility but would need careful handling on a public marketing page — you don't usually tell prospects your competitors are better at something. This is better as internal positioning guidance than marketing copy.
2. Missing: no mention of switching costs or migration path. A practice currently paying an agency wants to know "can I really replace them?"

### Videoscript
**Strengths:**
- Act 1 problem moment is instantly recognizable (Canva tabs, 2023 Facebook post, 0 likes). Sarah would see herself immediately.
- Act 2 walks through the actual product — not abstract.
- Timing is realistic (15s + 50s + 15s = 80s).
- Uses real case study data (Coastal Eye, 3,300 patients, 100+ appointments).

**Weaknesses found:**
1. Act 3 is generic. "Stop spending hours on marketing that doesn't work" is fine but doesn't match the energy of Act 1's specificity. Could end with a persona-specific hook.
2. The narrator voice is warm but could be more conversational. Lines like "Six hundred forty-seven practices are already running One-Click Campaigns" read like copy, not speech. Consider: "Over six hundred practices are already doing this."

### Screenshotbrief
**Strengths:**
- 7 shots covering the full product journey (selection → preview → results → social proof).
- Each shot has a clear "Why it matters" tied to a marketing claim.
- Data requirements explicitly call out NO SeaShore on every relevant shot.
- Persona connections are noted (Sarah needs Shot 2, Growth Chaser needs Shot 4, Dr. Someday needs Shot 7).

**Weaknesses found:**
1. Shot 7 (Network Admin Report) is marked "Optional" — this is actually one of the strongest social proof shots and should be required, not optional.
2. No shot specifically shows the scheduling/auto-posting calendar view (social posts distributing across the month). This is mentioned in the video script but not briefed as a screenshot.

## Part C — Process Critique

### Process Improvement #1
**File:** step5_competitive_research.md
**Issue:** Web research was almost entirely blocked by network egress restrictions. 4 of 5 template competitors and both specialized eye care competitors returned no results. Only Weave came back with data.
**Suggested change:** Add a note to Step 5 instructions: "If web research is blocked, rely on baseline competitor profiles, user-provided competitive intelligence, and training knowledge. Document which sources were accessible and which were not. The user's internal competitive framework (4-tier model) is often more valuable than competitor marketing copy."

### Process Improvement #2
**File:** step6_pregen_questions.md
**Issue:** The feature naming question (#6) required two rounds of brainstorming (12 options total). The Step 6 instructions don't suggest preparing name options in advance — this added a delay.
**Suggested change:** Add to Question 6 guidance: "If the internal feature name doesn't feel right for marketing, prepare 4-6 name options organized by vibe (literal, emotional, technical, branded) before asking the user. This reduces back-and-forth."

### Process Improvement #3
**File:** step4_resulting_view.md
**Issue:** The VIEW button on completed/ended campaigns shows a blank carousel (iframes don't render for finished campaigns). This caused a wrong-turn during Step 4.
**Suggested change:** Add to Step 4 instructions: "NOTE: The VIEW button on ended/completed campaigns may show a blank carousel because the preview iframes only render during active campaign setup. To see the patient-facing output, use the campaign setup flow on a new/active campaign — select a campaign from the library and cycle through the preview carousel there."
