## Killer Stat Candidates

### Primary: Scale / Social Proof
- **"647 practices ran campaigns in March 2026"** — proves widespread adoption
- **"3.7 million emails sent in a single month"** — volume at scale
- **"Over 10 million campaign emails in Q1 2026"** — quarterly proof

### Secondary: Engagement
- **"21% unique open rate network-wide (March 2026)"** — strong for automated campaigns
- **"62,000+ landing page views from campaign emails in one month"** — multi-channel reach
- **"Open rate improved from 18% to 21% Jan→Mar"** — trend improvement

### Cross-Reference with Step 1
Step 1 identified three killer stat types:
1. **Ghosting Rate** — % of patients who haven't been contacted → "39% of practices still not emailing" (alert metric) could be flipped: "61% of practices are actively emailing patients every month"
2. **Manual Labor Tax** — time saved → Not directly visible in admin data; need workflow comparison
3. **Capture Gap** — appointments attributed to campaigns → Per-practice appointment rates of 1-4% show attribution is working, with standout practices hitting 7-10%

**Recommended killer stat for page:** "647 practices sent 3.7 million campaign emails in March 2026 — and 21% were opened." Combines scale + engagement in one sentence.

## Feature-Specific Notes

### Report Structure
The Campaigns admin report (`/v2/a/reports/campaigns`) shows:
- **Summary cards:** Performance (Emails Sent, Unique Opens, Total Opens, Email Clicks, Page Views, EHR Email Appts) and Alerts (No Email, No Page Views, Bounce >5%, Complaints >0.1%)
- **Table columns:** Practice, Campaign, Social, Emails Sent, (?), Total Opens, Unique Opens, Bounces, Complaints, Email Clicks, Page Views, Appointments
- **Filters:** All Groups, All CSs/MMs, month/year picker
- **Pagination:** 100 per page, sorted by various columns
- **Export:** CSV download button available

### Data Observations
1. **39% of practices have "No Email"** — this is the alert metric. Nearly 4 in 10 practices on the platform are NOT sending campaign emails. This represents the "Ghosting Tax" opportunity from Step 1.
2. **Open rates vary widely** — from 12% to 57% depending on list size, list hygiene, and campaign relevance. Network average of 21% is solid for automated campaigns.
3. **Appointment attribution is dominated by open-attribution** — click rates are low (0.5-0.7%) but appointment rates are much higher (0.3-3.3%), confirming Step 1's note that open-attribution dominates click-attribution for EHR Email Appointments.
4. **Page views are an underused metric** — most practices show 0% page views, but practices like Delran Eye Assoc. (10%) show what's possible when the landing page component is properly leveraged.
5. **February dip is normal** — shorter month + post-holiday slowdown. March recovery is strong.
