## Competitive Landscape for Landing Pages

PatientEngage landing pages sit at a unique intersection: they are **conversion-optimized pages** (structured for appointment booking), **data-synced surfaces** (pulling live from the practice's PatientEngage data), and **multi-channel destinations** (serving campaigns, PPC, social ads, email, and any marketing effort that needs a focused booking page).

No competitor in the optometry space delivers a landing page that auto-syncs from the same source of truth as the practice's website, Google profile, Facebook page, and directory listings.

The competitive framework highlights two core vulnerabilities:
- **Data Drift** -- the landing page was accurate on day one and has quietly fallen out of sync with the practice's actual information
- **Conversion Disconnect** -- the landing page lacks the clinical trust signals (insurance logos, doctor bios, real reviews) that turn a visitor into a booked patient

## In-Industry Competitors

### Tebra (PatientPop) -- "The Template Trap"

They sell on all-in-one healthcare marketing.

- **The Problem:** Tebra offers website and landing page templates as part of their platform. But they're generalist healthcare templates. The pages are rigid, don't pull from a comprehensive practice data set (insurance logos, frame brands, contact lens brands, amenity badges), and don't auto-sync with directory listings or Google Business Profile. When a practice's hours change, the Tebra landing page and the Tebra website might update, but the 50+ directories and Google still show the old data unless managed separately.
- **What they do:** Patient scheduling, reputation management, website builder, some paid advertising. Landing pages are a feature within their website platform.
- **Their Edge:** All-in-one platform. Practice gets scheduling + reputation + website + landing pages in one vendor.
- **Our Counter:** Tebra pages are website pages repurposed as landing pages. They're not purpose-built for appointment conversion from paid traffic. And they don't sync with the broader digital footprint: directories, Google, Facebook. PatientEngage landing pages are one surface in a unified system where every channel reflects the same data.
- **Use case difference:** Tebra gives you a website with pages. PatientEngage gives you a conversion-optimized landing page that's one piece of a fully synced digital presence.

### iMatrix / Doctor Multimedia -- "Set It and Forget It"

They sell on low-cost website packages.

- **The Problem:** These providers build a website (and sometimes a landing page) during initial onboarding, then rarely touch it again. The landing page is a project, not a service. Six months later, the practice has a new doctor, changed hours, and added insurance plans. The landing page reflects none of it. Profile stagnation applies to landing pages just as much as it applies to Google Business Profiles.
- **What they do:** Website templates, initial SEO setup, basic online presence. Landing pages are sometimes included as part of the website build.
- **Their Edge:** Low price point. Practice feels like "my online presence is handled."
- **Our Counter:** A landing page that was accurate six months ago is a liability today. PatientEngage landing pages don't stagnate because they don't store content independently. They pull from live data. The content is always current because the source is always current.

### Thryv -- "Generic Pages for Generic Businesses"

They sell to all small businesses.

- **The Problem:** Thryv offers landing page tools as part of their small business platform. But these are template-based pages designed for plumbers, restaurants, and retail shops. They don't know what an optometry practice needs to show: insurance plan logos, doctor credentials and languages spoken, clinical services like Dry Eye or Myopia Management, patient testimonials from a review management system.
- **Our Counter:** An optometry landing page needs insurance proof, clinical credibility, and a scheduling pathway. Thryv can give you a form and a phone number. PatientEngage gives you 40+ insurance logos, doctor headshots with credentials, real patient reviews, and a scheduling integration that works with your existing system.

## Out-of-Industry: The "Silent" Competitors

### DIY / "I'll Just Use My Homepage" -- THE #1 RIVAL (The Status Quo)

The practice doesn't think they need a landing page.

- **The Problem:** Most practices send all traffic -- paid ads, social posts, email links -- to their homepage. The homepage has navigation menus, links to career pages, vendor information, and content designed for multiple audiences. A first-time visitor who searched "eye doctor near me" and clicked an ad lands on a page that asks them to figure out where to click next. They don't. They bounce.
- **Why this is #1:** Most practices don't compare PatientEngage landing pages to Unbounce or Leadpages. They compare it to "I'll just send people to my website." The answer isn't about features -- it's about showing why a focused page converts better than a general-purpose homepage.

### Unbounce / Leadpages / Instapage -- "The Build-It-Yourself Tools"

Drag-and-drop landing page builders.

- **What they do:** Visual editors for building landing pages from scratch. Templates, A/B testing, form builders, analytics. Powerful tools used across all industries.
- **Their Edge:** Full design control. A/B testing. Sophisticated analytics. Large template libraries.
- **Our Counter:** These are tools, not services. Someone at the practice (or their marketing agency) has to build the page, write the copy, add the images, and -- critically -- keep it updated when anything changes. The page is disconnected from the practice's data. Hours change? Someone has to manually update the Unbounce page. New doctor? Manual update. New insurance? Manual update. The tool is powerful, but it creates a maintenance burden and guarantees data drift over time. PatientEngage landing pages don't need to be built or maintained. They pull live data. No design skill required. No ongoing updates.
- **Use case difference:** Unbounce/Leadpages/Instapage are design tools for marketers. PatientEngage is a synced system for practices that don't have a marketer on staff.

### Generalist Agencies -- "The Marketing Guy"

The local agency who builds a landing page as part of a campaign package.

- **The Problem:** The agency builds a landing page for the campaign launch. It looks professional. Then the engagement ends (or the agency moves on to other clients), and the page becomes a time capsule. Nobody updates it. The agency doesn't have access to the practice's insurance list or team roster. They built the page with the information they had at the time, and that information is now 8 months old.
- **Our Counter:** An agency-built landing page is a snapshot. A PatientEngage landing page is a live feed. The agency page gets stale the moment the engagement ends. The PatientEngage page stays current because the data source stays current.

## The Objection Handlers

### Core Objection: "I spent $5,000 on my new website, shouldn't it be able to handle this?"

**Consultant Strategy:** "Your website is a beautiful lobby. It's for people who already know you. But a landing page is a closer. When we run a campaign for a $3,000 Scleral Lens fit, we need a specialist closer, not a general receptionist." [PROVIDED]

**Keenan Dagger:** "I'm confused. You said your goal was to add 20 new Myopia Management cases this quarter because the margins are higher. But you're sending that traffic to a homepage where the word 'Myopia' is hidden under a 'Services' tab. If you were a parent terrified about your kid's vision, and you had to play hide-and-seek to find help, would you stay on that site? Or would you click the next ad that actually speaks to you?" [PROVIDED]

**Page-Friendly Version:** Your website does a great job for patients who already know you. But when someone clicks an ad for "dry eye treatment near me," they need a page that speaks directly to that. A homepage with 15 menu options asks them to search for what they already told Google they wanted. A landing page mirrors the ad's promise at the top of the page and guides them straight to booking.

---

### 1. "We just send people to our website."

**Raw (Keenan):** "By sending ads to your homepage, you are intentionally choosing to lose 70% of your potential leads. We aren't asking you to spend more on ads; we're asking you to stop throwing away the traffic you've already paid for." [PROVIDED]

**Page-Friendly Version:** Your homepage converts about 2-3% of visitors. A dedicated landing page converts 6-10%, with top performers reaching 15% or higher. That's not a tweak -- it's a fundamental shift in how many of your paid clicks become appointments. The landing page doesn't replace your website. It makes your ad spend stop leaking.

### 2. "We already have a landing page."

**Page-Friendly Version:** When was it last updated? If your hours, team, or insurance list have changed since the page was built, visitors are seeing outdated information. PatientEngage landing pages pull live data from your practice profile, so they stay current without anyone remembering to update them. No data drift.

### 3. "I can build one myself with Unbounce/Wix/Squarespace."

**Page-Friendly Version:** You absolutely can. But who updates it when your hours change? When a new doctor joins? When you start accepting a new insurance plan? A self-built page is a snapshot of your practice on the day you built it. A PatientEngage landing page stays current because it pulls from the same data as your website, Google profile, and directory listings. And it comes with campaign-specific content that matches your marketing initiative.

### 4. "Landing pages are a waste of money."

**Raw (Keenan ROI Logic):** "Doctor, by sending ads to your homepage, you are intentionally choosing to lose 70% of your potential leads." [PROVIDED]

**Page-Friendly Version:** If you're running ads, emails, or social campaigns, you're already paying for every click. The question is where those clicks land. A homepage with 40 links and 1 goal converts at 2-3%. A landing page with 1 link and 1 goal converts at 6-10%. The landing page isn't the expense. It's what stops your existing marketing spend from going to waste.

### 5. "Our marketing agency handles this."

**Page-Friendly Version:** Your agency builds great pages. But do they have access to your current insurance list? Your updated team roster? Your latest hours? Agency-built pages are only as current as the last time the agency asked for updates. PatientEngage landing pages pull the data directly, so the page is always current even between agency check-ins.

### 6. "We don't run ads, so we don't need a landing page."

**Page-Friendly Version:** Landing pages aren't just for paid ads. They're the conversion destination for email campaigns, social media posts, seasonal promotions, and any time you want to drive patients to a focused booking page. Anytime you share a link and want it to end in an appointment, a landing page outperforms your homepage.

### 7. "It's too complicated to set up."

**Page-Friendly Version:** There's nothing to set up. Your practice data already exists in PatientEngage: hours, team, insurance, reviews, location. The landing page pulls from what's already there. Campaign-specific content is created to match each marketing initiative. No page builder to learn. No content to write. No images to hunt down.

Hours, team, insurance, reviews: all pulled live. When anything changes, the landing page reflects it. No extra updates. No extra logins. No data drift. Every click lands on a page that's current, complete, and optimized for one thing: booking the appointment."
