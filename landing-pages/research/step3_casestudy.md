# Case Study: Landing Pages -- The Data Drift Problem

## Why This Case Study Is Different

Landing Pages doesn't have network-wide conversion stats to lead with. The value story has two pillars: **eliminating data drift** and **maximizing appointment conversions**. The landing page is the conversion destination for campaigns, PPC, social ads, email blasts, or any service that needs a focused booking page. It's optimized for one thing: turning a visitor into a booked appointment. And it stays current automatically because it pulls live from the same data the practice already maintains in PatientEngage.

## Case Study: "Every Click Deserves a Page That's Ready to Convert"

### The Problem: Data Drift

A practice builds a landing page for their Google Ads campaign. It looks great on day one. Then life happens:

- The practice changes their Wednesday hours. The website gets updated. Google gets updated through PatientEngage. The landing page? Still showing the old hours. Nobody remembered to update it.
- A new doctor joins the team. Their photo and bio go on the website. The landing page still shows only the original two doctors.
- The practice starts accepting three new insurance plans. The website's insurance page gets updated. The landing page still shows the old list. A patient with one of the new plans sees the ad, clicks through, doesn't see their insurance listed, and leaves.
- The Saturday hours change for summer. The website reflects it. The landing page shows winter hours through July.

Each of these mismatches is small. But together, they create a credibility gap. The patient sees one thing on the ad, something different on the landing page, and something else on Google. That inconsistency -- even if it's just hours being off by 30 minutes -- is enough to make a new patient pick the competitor whose information looks consistent.

**The worst part:** nobody at the practice knows. There's no alert that says "your landing page shows different hours than your Google profile." The data just drifts, silently, for months or years.

### The Impact: Wasted Traffic From Every Channel

Landing pages aren't just for Google Ads. They're the conversion destination for PPC campaigns, social media ads, email blasts, seasonal promotions, and any marketing effort that needs a focused booking page. Every one of those channels sends traffic to this page expecting it to convert visitors into appointments.

When the page shows stale data, it doesn't matter which channel drove the click. A patient who arrives from a Facebook campaign and sees outdated hours bounces just as fast as one who clicked a Google Ad. The traffic source did its job -- it got the visitor to the page. The landing page failed because it showed wrong hours, a missing doctor, or an outdated insurance list. The practice blames the campaigns. The real problem is the page the campaigns point to.

Google Ads for optometry keywords alone can run $5-15+ per click in competitive markets [ESTIMATE]. Add social ad spend, email campaign costs, and the staff time spent on promotions, and the practice is investing thousands of dollars a month driving traffic to a page they haven't checked since it was built.

- The average landing page conversion rate across industries is approximately 5.9% [ESTIMATE -- Unbounce, 2024]

### The Manual Alternative

Building and maintaining a landing page without PatientEngage means:

1. **Choose a tool:** Unbounce, Leadpages, Instapage, or have a web developer build one. Cost: $80-200/month for landing page tools, or $500-2,000+ for custom development.
2. **Write the content:** Copy the practice description, doctor bios, insurance list, hours, and services from the website. Or write new content from scratch.
3. **Add images:** Find or create doctor headshots, practice photos, insurance logos. Format them for the page.
4. **Set up scheduling:** Embed a booking widget or create a form. Connect it to the practice's scheduling system.
5. **Launch:** Page goes live. Looks great.
6. **Month 2:** Hours change. Someone needs to update both the website AND the landing page.
7. **Month 4:** New doctor joins. Someone needs to update both the website AND the landing page.
8. **Month 6:** Nobody has updated the landing page since launch. It shows winter hours in June. The insurance section is missing four plans. One of the two doctors listed has left the practice.
9. **Month 12:** The practice wonders why their ad conversion rate dropped. Nobody connects it to the stale landing page.

Estimated ongoing maintenance: 2-4 hours per month if someone actually remembers to do it. At $22/hour, that's $528-1,056 per year in staff time. In reality, most practices don't maintain it at all.

## What PatientEngage Does: A Landing Page That Maintains Itself

### The Architecture

PatientEngage landing pages are not built from scratch. They are assembled from data the practice already maintains in the platform:

| Landing Page Section | PatientEngage Source | Updates Automatically? |
|---|---|---|
| Practice name, address, tagline | Locations | Yes |
| Google Map + amenity badges | Google integration + Locations | Yes |
| Weekly hours + special dates | Hours | Yes |
| About Us + services list | About Us | Yes |
| Practice photos | Image gallery | Yes |
| Doctor headshots, bios, credentials | Our Team | Yes |
| Insurance plans with logos | Insurance | Yes |
| Patient reviews | Testimonials | Yes |
| Scheduling | Scheduler / Form / Third-party link | Configured once |
| Tracking phone number | Campaign setup | Configured once |
| Announcements | Announcements | Yes |
| Frame brands | Frames | Yes |
| Contact lens brands | Contact Lenses | Yes |

**The key insight:** The practice is already entering this data into PatientEngage for their website, their Google Business Profile, their Facebook page, and their directory listings. The landing page is another surface that reflects the same source of truth. When any section is updated, every surface that uses it -- website, Google, Facebook, directories, AND landing pages -- reflects the change.

### What This Means in Practice

- Hours change? Update once in PatientEngage. The landing page, website, Google, Facebook, and 50+ directories all reflect the new hours.
- New doctor joins? Add their bio to Our Team. The landing page shows them. The website shows them.
- New insurance plan accepted? Add it to Insurance. The landing page adds the logo. The website adds the listing.
- New patient review comes in? The landing page testimonial carousel updates.

**Zero additional maintenance for the landing page.** The practice maintains their data in PatientEngage (which they're already doing), and the landing page stays current as a byproduct.

### Optimized for Appointment Conversions

The landing page isn't a data display. Every section exists to move a visitor toward one action: booking an appointment.

- **Persistent header:** Phone CTA + Book button always visible
- **Hero with location context:** Practice name, city-specific tagline, friendly address description
- **Trust signals early:** Hours, Google Map, amenity badges (Same-Day Appointments, Walk-Ins Accepted, Wheelchair Accessible)
- **Social proof:** Real patient reviews in a carousel
- **Credibility:** Doctor headshots with bios, insurance logos with "Show All" for comprehensive lists
- **Multiple conversion points:** Phone CTA + Book Online button repeated throughout the page
- **Flexible scheduling:** PatientEngage Scheduler, contact form, or third-party scheduler link

## The "Data Drift" Frame

The practice thinks their landing page is fine. They built it (or had it built) last year and it looked great. They haven't looked at it since.

**Current State:** The landing page shows winter hours in June. It lists two doctors but a third joined in March. The insurance section is missing the four new plans they started accepting. The reviews are the same five from a year ago.

**The Impact:** Every ad click costs real money. A patient who sees outdated hours or doesn't see their insurance listed leaves. They don't call to ask. They just click back and choose the next result. The practice's ad spend is leaking through a page they forgot about.

**The Root Cause:** The landing page is disconnected from the practice's data. It was a snapshot of the practice on the day it was built. Everything changed. The page didn't.

**The Cost of Inaction:** "You're spending $2,000 a month on Google Ads. If even 10% of those clicks bounce because the landing page shows wrong hours or a missing doctor, that's $200/month wasted. Not because the ads failed, but because the page they land on is out of date."
